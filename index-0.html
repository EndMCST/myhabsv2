    Understanding Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs)

    Harmful Algal Blooms (HABs) are rapid growths of algae that can produce toxins, deplete oxygen levels, and harm aquatic life. These blooms are often triggered by excess nutrients, rising temperatures, and environmental changes.

    HABs pose a significant threat to ecosystems, public health, and local economies. Monitoring and early detection are crucial in mitigating their effects.

    • Threat to Marine Life
    • Public Health Risk
    • Economic Impact

    Using IoT sensors and data analysis, we provide real-time monitoring and predictive insights to detect and mitigate HABs, ensuring healthier aquatic environments.
